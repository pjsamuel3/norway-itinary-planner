Saturday · 23 May

Arrival & Airport

Landing at 21:45 — priority is easy and close. Three options at or near Gardermoen.

🏨

Radisson Blu Airport Hotel HOST REC

Short covered walk directly to/from the terminal — no taxi needed with tired kids and luggage. Reliable, comfortable, good breakfast. Best for an early morning flight out.

📍 Google Maps ☎️ +47 63 93 30 00
🏨

Radisson Red Oslo Airport HOST REC

More modern and design-led, ~5 min walk to the terminal. Stylish rooms, great if you want something a bit hipper. Both Radissons are equally good options.

📍 Google Maps ☎️ +47 67 02 41 00
🏨

Comfort Hotel Runway BUDGET

Requires a shuttle bus (80 NOK/person). Significantly cheaper, breakfast from 4am. Less convenient late at night with young kids, but worth considering if price is the priority.

Tuesday · Oslo Day 1

City by Bike

🚲

E-bike through the city

Borrowing our electric bike — it fits both kids on the back. Suggested route: Vigeland Park → waterfront → Opera House. Best done in the morning before it gets busy.

🌿

Vigeland Park

Free, open 24 hours. Gustav Vigeland's monumental sculpture park — enormous and great for kids to roam. One of Oslo's true unmissables: 200+ bronze and granite sculptures.

📍 Google Maps 🕐 Always open · Free
🎭

Oslo Opera House

Walk up the sloping white marble roof for panoramic fjord views. The architecture alone is worth the trip. Free to explore outside — kids will love running up the roof.

📍 Google Maps 🕐 Roof always open · Free

Wednesday · Oslo Day 2

Heights & Forests

🏔️

Holmenkollen Ski Jump

Take the T-bane (Metro line 1) up to Holmenkollen. Ride to the top of the jump for extraordinary views over Oslo and the fjord. Ski museum on site — history dating back to the 1860s.

📍 Google Maps 🕐 Daily 10:00–16:00
🌲

Hiking from Holmenkollen

Marked trails lead directly into the Nordmarka forest from the jump. Easy, well-signposted paths — manageable for a 6 and 9 year old. Beautiful Norwegian forest in late May.

👁️

Grefsenkollen Viewpoint

Alternative or add-on viewpoint — panoramic Oslo view from a different angle, accessible by a separate Metro line. Could combine with Teknisk Museum nearby.

🔬

Norsk Teknisk Museum

100+ hands-on exhibits including a science centre, oil rig simulator, model railway, aviation hall and more. Outstanding for kids and adults alike — genuinely one of the best science museums in Scandinavia.

🕐 Tue–Fri 09:00–16:00 · Closed Mon 🔗 tekniskmuseum.no 📍 Google Maps
🚶

Maridalsvatnet

Oslo's main reservoir — lovely, easy lakeside walk, very manageable for young kids. Beautiful in late May when the trees are fully out. Quiet and peaceful, far from tourist crowds.

📍 Google Maps

Thursday · Oslo Day 3

Waterfront Day

⚓

Aker Brygge

Oslo's main waterfront district. Great for a morning wander, lunch, coffee, watching the harbour traffic. Central hub for the day — everything else is a short walk or ferry from here.

📍 Google Maps
🛁

KOK Floating Sauna

Wood-fired floating sauna moored at Aker Brygge — heat up, then plunge into the fjord. One of Oslo's most memorable experiences. Book in advance.

⚠️ Check kid policy when booking

🕐 Daily from 07:00 📍 Google Maps
🏰

Akershus Fortress

13th century fortress on the fjord — free to enter, cobbled grounds, excellent harbour views. Easy ten-minute walk from Aker Brygge. Kids like the cannon mounts.

🕐 Daily 06:00–21:00 · Free 📍 Google Maps
🎨

Tjuvholmen

Adjacent to Aker Brygge — sculpture beach, Astrup Fearnley modern art museum, striking contemporary architecture. A pleasant fifteen-minute wander from the waterfront.

📍 Google Maps
🚢

Fram Museum

Take ferry Line 91 from Aker Brygge. Board the Fram — the world's strongest wooden ship, used in Arctic and Antarctic polar expeditions. Genuinely immersive. One of the best things in Oslo for kids.

🕐 Daily 10:00–17:00 📍 Google Maps
🖼️

Munch Museum

Home of The Scream and thousands of other Munch works. World-class, beautifully designed building with fjord views. Free entry on Wednesday evenings — plan accordingly.

🕐 Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 · Wed–Sat until 21:00 📍 Google Maps
