Tue · 27 May · Oslo Day 1

City by Bike

Sculptures, rooftops and the fjord from two wheels

🚲

E-bike through the city

⭐ Kids love it

Borrowing our electric bike — fits both kids on the back. Suggested loop: Vigeland Park → waterfront → Opera House → back. Best before the city fills up in the morning.

⭐⭐⭐Both kids will want to steer
🌿

Vigeland Park

Free⭐ Kids love it

Over 200 monumental bronze and granite sculptures by Gustav Vigeland set in a vast green park — open all hours. Kids will run riot and love every second. One of Oslo's unmissables.

🕐 Always open🎟️ Free
📍 Map
⭐⭐⭐Giant naked statues — instantly iconic for kids
🎭

Oslo Opera House

Free outside

Walk up the sloping white marble roof for panoramic fjord views. The architecture alone is worth the detour. Kids love running up the inclined roof — it feels like walking on water.

🕐 Roof always open
📍 Map
⭐⭐⭐Running on a rooftop above the sea — magical
Wed · 28 May · Oslo Day 2

Heights & Forest

Ski jumps, forest trails and hands-on science

🏔️

Holmenkollen Ski Jump

⭐ Kids love it

Take the T-bane (Metro line 1) to Holmenkollen. Ride to the top of the jump for extraordinary views over Oslo and the fjord. Ski museum on site — history from Viking skis to Olympic gold.

🕐 Daily 10:00–16:00🚇 T-bane line 1
📍 Map
⭐⭐⭐Standing at the top is genuinely vertiginous
🌲

Hiking from Holmenkollen

Free

Marked trails lead directly into Nordmarka forest from the jump. Easy, well-signposted paths — manageable for a 6 and 9 year old. Beautiful Norwegian forest in late May, fully green.

⭐⭐Great energy burner after the ski jump
👁️

Grefsenkollen Viewpoint

Alternative or add-on — panoramic Oslo view from a different Metro line. Pairs well with Teknisk Museum nearby for a focused afternoon without backtracking.

🔬

Norsk Teknisk Museum

⭐ Kids love it

100+ hands-on exhibits — science centre, oil rig simulator, model railway, aviation hall. One of the best science museums in Scandinavia. Adults enjoy it just as much as kids.

🕐 Tue–Fri 09:00–16:00⚠️ Closed Mondays
🔗 Website 📍 Map
⭐⭐⭐Oil rig simulator is a genuine highlight
🚶

Maridalsvatnet

Free

Oslo's main reservoir — an easy lakeside walk in a quiet setting far from tourist crowds. Beautiful in late May when everything is green. Very manageable for young kids.

📍 Map
Thu · 29 May · Oslo Day 3

Waterfront Day

Saunas, polar ships and The Scream

⚓

Aker Brygge

The day's base camp. Oslo's main waterfront district — morning coffee, harbour watching, good lunch options. Walk west to Tjuvholmen; take the ferry east for museums.

📍 Map
🛁

KOK Floating Sauna

Book ahead

Wood-fired floating sauna moored at Aker Brygge — heat up, then plunge into the fjord. One of Oslo's most memorable experiences. Book well in advance in summer.

⚠️ Check kid age policy when booking

🕐 Daily from 07:00
📍 Map
🏰

Akershus Fortress

Free⭐ Kids love it

13th century fortress on the fjord — free to enter, cobbled grounds, cannon mounts, excellent harbour views. Easy walk from Aker Brygge. Kids love exploring the battlements.

🕐 Daily 06:00–21:00🎟️ Free
📍 Map
⭐⭐Cannons and cobblestones — kids approved
🎨

Tjuvholmen

Adjacent to Aker Brygge — sculpture beach, Astrup Fearnley contemporary art museum, striking modern architecture. A pleasant 15-minute wander west of the waterfront.

📍 Map
🚢

Fram Museum

⭐ Kids love it

Take ferry Line 91 from Aker Brygge. Board the Fram — the world's strongest wooden ship, used in Arctic and Antarctic polar expeditions. Ice tunnels, dramatic audio, genuine wonder. The best thing in Oslo for kids.

🕐 Daily 10:00–17:00⛴️ Ferry Line 91
📍 Map
⭐⭐⭐Polar exploration on a real ship — unforgettable
🖼️

Munch Museum

Home of The Scream and thousands of other Munch works. World-class, beautifully designed building with fjord views. Free entry on Wednesday evenings — worth rearranging days for.

🕐 Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00🕐 Wed–Sat until 21:00
📍 Map
